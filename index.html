Orlando Mendiola

Orlando is a senior at The New School and majors in Journalism + Design with a minor in photography at Parsons School of Design. He has developed a passion for storytelling through photography. He believes every photo has meaning and aims to capture his life and subjects in their truest form.

Photo Stories

The Ties That Bind: NYC and the Spots that We Bind To

June 2, 2018

This past summer I took a two-week photography intensive workshop at NYU. I studied under photojournalist Ron Haviv and editor Lauren Walsh. The theme of the class was “The Ties That Bind.” We were given creative freedom to interpret what that meant for our photo assignments. I interpreted it as the places that bind us to the city. I documented five of my friends and photographed them at places that tie them to the city.

March For Our Lives: Student-Led Protests Against Gun Violence Draw Hundreds of Thousands

March 24, 2018

I covered the March for Our Lives: NYC for The Trace, an independent news publication dedicated to expanding coverage of guns in the United States. The article was a collaborative effort with other photographers from across the country who also covered the march. My photos appear about halfway down.

Pizza

May 6, 2016

This project was for my "Reporting Without Words," class. This class was a printmaking and journlaism class. We had to create an 8-part story using only images and no words. I hand carved eight 4X6 linoleum blocks to create my story which was about a local pizza shop that increased their dollar slices to $2 and then back to $1 after they started to lose buisness.
